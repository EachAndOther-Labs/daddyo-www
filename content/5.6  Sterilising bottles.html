Sterilising bottles

Sterilising your baby's feeding equipment is vital in preventing potential infections.
You can either use a sterilising solution or an electric steriliser, which is probably more convenient but is also more expensive.

Sterilising solution Workstation

  • Used bottles and teats

  • Sterilising solution

  • Bottle brush

  • Cold water

  • Big container

  • Floating cover for the container

The Job

  1. Using the bottle brush, wash the bottle and the teat in hot soapy water, the sooner after a feed the better.
  2. Rinse everything in cold water.
  3. Mix the sterilising solution with cold water in the container, following the solution manufacturer's instructions on the label.
  4. Place the bottle and the teat in the solution, making sure there's no air trapped in the bottle.
  5. Use the floating cover to keep everything submerged.
  6. Leave everything in the solution for at least 30 minutes.
  7. Change the solution every day.

Electric steriliser Workstation

  • Used bottles and teats

  • The steriliser

The Job

  1. Using the bottle brush, wash the bottle and the teat in hot soapy water, the sooner after a feed the better.
  2. Rinse everything in cold water.
  3. Place all the equipment in the steriliser with openings and/or teats facing downwards.
  4. Follow the manufacturer's guidelines on how long to leave everything in the steriliser.

PRO TIP

In a pinch you can sterilise by boiling the equipment in water. However, this will eventually damage the teats and Daddyo recommends avoiding it. If you are boiling to sterilise, make sure that you repeatedly wash your hands and use the bottle directly after sterilisation. In general, the other two methods above offer better safety and convenience.
