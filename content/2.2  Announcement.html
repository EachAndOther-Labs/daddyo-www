Announcement

When to announce

The tradition is to wait until twelve weeks from conception before telling your family and friends that you're going to have a baby.

It's more than tradition. The majority of miscarriages happen in the first three months. It can be hard enough to cope with this as a couple without having the added pressure of telling the bad news to everyone. So resist the temptation as much as possible.

Many couples make the choice not to tell the big news until after their first scan. You'll both probably be really excited to spread the word as quickly as possible but to avoid hurt feelings tell agree together to tell people in order.

Assuming that you're lucky enough to have all of the following, The Daddyo recommended announcement order is:

Announcement order

  • 1. Both sets of parents

  • 2. Brothers, sisters and close friends

  • 3. Aunts, uncles, cousins

  • 4. Colleagues & anyone else.

If you feel the need to share your news over Facebook, only do it after you've gone through this list. Family members might find it hurtful to find out third hand that they're going to have a new relation.

PRO TIP

Book your paternity leave/holidays from work. You may need to ask for some flexibility, as the birth could happen a couple of weeks before or after the due date and you might have to duck out on very short notice. Nevertheless, having these dates down in the work diary makes everything feel a lot more real.
