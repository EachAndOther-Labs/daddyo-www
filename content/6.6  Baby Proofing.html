Baby Proofing

Baby Proofing your home

The good news is that your baby won't be too mobile in the first six months. But it will get mobile soon, and possibly unpredictably.

Make sure that your living room, kitchen, nursery and any other areas that your baby is likely to spend time in, are generally safe. No unstable book shelves, piles of art materials, Xboxes with wires that you could trip on - look at your place with a critical eye.

Later on you'll need to go much further in providing a safe environment for your kid. But here are the basics...

Install Alarms

    Smoke alarm

  • At least one on each level of the house.

    • Carbon Monoxide alarm

  • Make sure that it's fitted to the manufacturer's guidelines.

If you already have alarms installed, now is a good time to have them checked.

Around the house

    Safety gates

  • At the top and bottom of the stairs.

    • Plastic caps

  • For electrical sockets.

    • Child proof door locks

  • For both house doors and internal doors.

    • Medicines

  • Any that you'd commonly leave around on tables should be secured away. That goes for multivitamins too.

Nursery

    Thick carpet

  • Or a rug on the floor for bumps and falls.

    • Night light

  • So you can see where you're going in the dark.

    • Finger pinch guards

  • So doors won't close on their hands.
