Cravings

What are they?

Pregnancy is often associated with food cravings. They often have a sudden onset and can be quite intense. It could be a craving for something that your partner normally likes, normally doesn't like, or something that's downright weird. (Alternatively, your partner might go off foods that she used to love.)

You can make them work for you - a well-timed trip to an Indian takeaway for a curry craving can earn you a lot of good will.

What causes them?

There's no consensus on what causes cravings. They usually start in the first trimester, are at their strongest in the second, and have generally subsided before the birth of the baby.

It's common for women to crave dairy products, sweet foods or salty foods including pickles. Unless it's explicitly against medical advice, your partner should feel free to satisfy them. Stock the fridge and have takeaway menus to hand.

PRO TIP

Any cravings your partner normally has are likely to continue, whether they're harmful (nicotine), or not so harmful (chocolate). Obviously, the harmful cravings should be ignored.
