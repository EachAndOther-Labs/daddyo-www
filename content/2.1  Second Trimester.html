Second Trimester

The Baby

From week 12th to week 18-20. The baby is now putting on about 100g a week in weight, and in the last few weeks of the trimester the baby will be almost fully formed. It'll also start being very active in the womb. Expect the first kick sometime between weeks 16 and 20.

Some women experience feotal movements even earlier, which are called quickening or flutters.

Your Partner

The good news is that your partner's morning sickness has probably eased off. However, she's still likely to suffer from heartburn, indigestion as well as constipation, varicose veins, hemorrhoids and general aches and pains. Yes, the going can get tough.

Your partner's body is going through a lot of changes in a very short space of time, so as she's getting bigger she may get some stretch marks. She might be self-conscious or unhappy about all of this. Do a good job of making her feel good about herself. Most stretch marks reduce after giving birth.

The bump will start showing and as the morning sickness and other symptoms subside, your partner may find she's enjoying this trimester more than the first. So you should too.

Milestones

  • Telling your family and friends

  • Flutters

  • First kick

Warning signs

  • Unusual abdominal pain

  • Vaginal bleeding

  • Chronic nausea or vomiting
