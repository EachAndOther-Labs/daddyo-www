Missing due date

Why are babies born late?

Labour usually starts when the time is right. Every pregnancy is different and a late birth, especially the first time around, isn't uncommon. Your baby isn't officially late until two weeks after the actual due date.
Unless there's a clear indication that the baby is in trouble of some sort, your hospital or midwife will probably recommend letting nature take its course.

However, you're probably pretty fed up with everything at this point, so there are some things that you can try, which vary from the science based to the old-wives tales.

None of them are reliable, so occupy your mind and find something useful to do:

What to do?

Cook

Use the extra time to cook a lot of freezer ready food. You'll be congratulating yourself on your foresight after the birth.

Plan stuff to do

Box sets, walks, evenings out, friends over, books, video games. Do things together, and with other people. No need to get too adventurous - your partner could still go into labour at any time.

Relax

Send your partner off for a gentle massage somewhere or run her a bath. And if you have the time, catch up on sleep.

Work

Alternatively, work. If you've got a lot on your plate, doing more before the birth might give you a little extra space after it.

What helps to bring on labour?

Not much. There's a lot of advice out there on the internet. It's all pretty much nonsense. Some of it is benign, some of it is ill-advised and some of it is ridiculous. Stick to the following:

Sex

Semen contains prostaglandin which softens the cervis. Sex also causes the release of oxytocin, which is linked to contractions. Don't try this if your partner's waters have already broken.

Light Exercise

A walk around a park might work. Even if it doesn't, at least you got out.
