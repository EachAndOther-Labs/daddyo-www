Solid foods

Weaning and solid foods

Introducing solids into your baby's diet is called weaning, and it starts at about six months. Before this point, their digestive system just isn't developed enough to handle anything other than breast milk or baby formula.

Even though you start introducing solids you should still continue to bottle or breastfeed.

Workstation

  • Bowl + spoon

  • Food

  • Bib

  • Highchair

  • Baby wipes/cloth

  • Microwave/stove

Judging the amount of food is hard at first. A baby at four months can eat anywhere between 1-3 tablespoons of food at 1-2 'meals', in addition to bottles or breastfeeding. By six months, it'll be around 4 teaspoons, 3 times a day. The guideline is, feed until the kid has had enough. You'll get to know your baby's appetite.

The Job

  • Heat the food up in the microwave or on the stove.

  • Taste it to check the temperature. It should be warm, but not too warm.

  • Put the baby in the high chair and make sure that it's secure.

  • Fasten the bib comfortably around the neck; this is going to get messy.

  • Scoop small amounts of baby food onto the tip of the baby spoon.

  • Take off the bib.

  • Using the baby wipes, clean their hands and face.

  • Take the baby out of the high chair and put it somewhere safe.

  • Give the high chair a good wipe down.

NEVER

  • Leave your baby alone in the highchair.

Weaning

The baby should always be sitting up straight and facing you

Be regular. Keep meal times close to the same time every day.

Feeding the baby when you and your partner are also eating can help get their interest. When babies see you eating they tend to want some too.

Start introducing food by offering few teaspoons a day or a single piece of soft fruit

Let the baby touch and hold the food to get used to it.

Make sure the the temperature is correct.

Take your time. You'll need some patience

PRO TIP

Nothing beats the aeroplane maneuver.

Warning Sign

You'll get to know your baby's feeding patterns. If it refuses to eat or shows no interest in food over the course of a couple of days, you should contact your GP.

PRO TIP

Fresh is best. Try not to rely on processed baby food except for emergencies. You've already learned how to cook during your partner's pregnancy, now learn to cook healthy, tasty baby food.
