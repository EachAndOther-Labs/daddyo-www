Preparing a bottle

Workstation

  • Clean worktop

  • Sterilised bottle + teat

  • Formula

  • Kettle

  • Fresh tap/drinking water

The Job

  1. Boil the kettle. Use fresh tap/drinking water each time.
  2. Let it cool for 30 minutes.
  3. Follow the guidelines on the formula container.
  4. Fill the bottle with the exact amount of water required.
  5. Add in exactly the amount of powder recommend on the formula container.
  6. Put on the lid and shake it until it's well mixed.
  7. Fill a bowl or other container with hot water but don't fill it to the top of the container.
  8. Put the bottle in the container to heat it up. ONLY use this method to heat the bottle. No microwaves. Ever.
  9. Test the heat of the milk on your wrist. It should feel a little warm but not hot.
  10. Position the baby comfortably in your arms. Her head should be higher than her body.
  11. Hold the bottle at an angle so that the teat is filled with milk. Put it fully into her mouth.
  12. She may stop and start feeding several times. Take your time and do your best to get her to take the whole bottle.
  13. If she's done feeding, get rid of any formula that she did take. There's too much of a risk of bacteria.
  14. Feeding should take about 20 minutes but that's a guideline not a hard and fast rule.

PRO TIP

Timers are your friend. Set countdown on your phone for 30 mins as soon as you turn the kettle on.

PRO TIP

Change you holding position a few times during the feed. This helps keep the baby focused on the task at hand and it'll give you arms a bit of a break.

PRO TIP

Get a few formula holders. Fill them with the right amount of powder you need. Fill a few bottles with boiled and cooled water. Now you have a baby feeding travel pack; all you need it a friendly person to give you a container of hot water to heat up the bottle.

Warning Signs

    Refusing to feed

  • This might be OK, or a sign that something is wrong. If the baby has missed two or three feedings and seems uncomfortable, it's probably best to head to your GP.

    • Vomiting

  • Babies vomit a lot. This is often due to swallowing air during feeding. They could also have a bout of gastroenteritis, which they should fight off in a day or two. If you baby is vomiting, monitor its temperature and contact your GP if you're concerned. Persistent vomiting can be a sign of something more serious, including meningitis or an allergy. Be cool, but be vigilant.
