Breast or bottle Feeding

Breast feeding is generally considered the best way to go but there can be good reasons why your partner might choose to bottle feed instead.

Breast - Pros

  • Health benefits for baby and mother.

  • Helps mothers lose pregnancy weight.

  • It's efficient for waste and there's much fewer nappy changes.

Breast - Cons

  • It can be painful for your partner - her nipples can get sore or her breasts can become engorged.

  • Risk of mastitis.

Bottle - Pros

  • Anyone can feed the baby.

  • None of the downsides to breast feeding.

Bottle - Cons

  • None of the upsides to breast feeding.

  • Babies are more prone to colic (intense crying).

  • A lot of stuff to carry around. Although, you probably will switch to bottle at some point so this is only a short term con.

PRO TIP

Breast milk can be pumped and stored for feeding by bottle. Which means that you can get a turn, Daddyo. Introduce this at around four weeks. It's much harder to get an older baby to start with bottle feeding.

PRO TIP

If in doubt as to what you want to do, best to start with breastfeeding as you can always switch to bottle feed afterwards.
