Finances

Babies are expensive. But how much you spend is totally up to you and your partner. Like buying a car, you can get a fully kitted out Porsche, or go for an ultra-reliable Toyota that will get the job done just as well.

As a general rule, don't get carried away. There are lots of good quality, good value products out there. Do some market research online and shop around.

Whether you do a budget now or not, you will need to do one for the next year.

Regular chats

You and your partner should set aside time to talk about the figures. Once a month, once every two weeks. Whatever is needed. Stick the dates in the diary and keep to them. You could hold these meet ups in a caf√© or a quiet bar if that makes them less painful.

PRO TIP

Saving from day one is a good idea. Even if you've been planning for a while, use the nine months to get a budget in place that allows you to put a few quid away. Not everyone can afford to do so, but if you can it'll take some pressure off later.

PRO TIP

People don't need buggies and baby gear forever. Savvy shoppers can pick up great deals on second hand gear, and hand-me-downs are likely to be offered by friends and family. Just make sure that you are satisfied that the items are safe and in good nick. Never buy a second hand car seat.

PRO TIP

Make a list of all the major purchases and buy one of them every month for the duration of the pregnancy. It'll be weird buying a bottle steriliser six months in advance but do it now and you won't have a giant last minute panic-shop.
