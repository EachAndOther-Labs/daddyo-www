Taking baby home

Welcome home checklist

Here's what you'll need to make homecoming comfortable for your partner and your new arrival.

Your partner

  • Clothes - Clean and loose fitting

  • PJs - Several pairs

  • Maternity towels - Several packs

  • Underwear - Clean and comfortable

  • Hemorrhoid clean and wipes (just buy it and don't ask questions)

  • Squirty bottle - Can help with bathing and relieve stinging

  • Lanolin - If breastfeeding. Your can get this is in the chemist

  • Ice packs - For swelling

Baby

  • Nursery - Kitted out, clean and tidy

  • Feeding equipment - Bottles, steriliser, formula if bottle feeding

  • Clothes - Suitable for newborns

  • Nappies - Suitable for newborns

  • Baby clothes - Including onesies

  • Baby wipes

House

Make sure that the house is clean, warm and in good shape.

Food

Have at least a week's worth of food in the fridge and freezer.

Car seat

Properly installed and ready for use.

Family visits

If you have lots of family and friends, make a staggered plan for them to visit so they don't all show up the moment you get home. You might want to leave the first day free for just the three of you.

Suit yourselves on this one but try to strike a balance between welcoming people in and giving you and your partner some space. You should be the gatekeeper on this one but prioritise (the new) grandparents, siblings and closest friends. Don't let anyone with cold or flu symptoms around newborns.
